01
Executive Summary

This document proposes a 30-day controlled pilot of the Valterra SLAM Re-Entry Application — a purpose-built digital safety system that guides Overseer Miners through the complete re-entry procedure following primary blasts at Unki Mine.

The application digitises Procedure UNK-MIN-MIN-PRO-0002 Version 6.0 in its entirety. A fully functional system has been developed, tested, and reviewed by senior engineering personnel who responded positively. The backend database is connected, the SHE management dashboard is operational, and the system has passed a 72-point technical audit.

Pilot objective: Validate the application in a real underground environment alongside existing paper forms for 30 days. Zero disruption to current operations — paper forms remain in use throughout. The pilot produces a data-driven recommendation on full mine-wide deployment.
02
Problem Statement

The current paper-based SLAM re-entry procedure presents compliance and safety risks that have been observed directly in underground operations. These are consistent failure modes of paper-based safety systems across the mining industry.

ProblemCurrent ImpactApplication Solution
Retrospective completionForms completed after the fact, not in real timeEach step confirmed live — next step is locked until current is done
Critical steps skippableNo enforcement mechanism under time pressureHard phase locking — critical steps cannot be bypassed by any means

03
Application Overview

The SLAM Re-Entry Application is a mobile-first web application accessible from any smartphone browser — no installation required. It was built directly from Procedure UNK-MIN-MIN-PRO-0002 Version 6.0.

PhaseSectionKey Enforcement
Phase 19.1 Re-Entry Preparation2-hour lapse automatically calculated and enforced. SCADA fan check, blasting board confirmation — all critical, all locked.
Phase 29.2 Waiting Place ManagementSafety Talk topics recorded, crew members captured by name and role, PPE inspection, fit-for-duty confirmation.
Phase 39.3 Initial Re-Entry30-minute countdown timer mandatory before advancing. Gas readings (CO/NO₂/O₂/H₂S) validated against legal limits. FOG light status — red triggers immediate ground alert and hard-blocks procedure.
Phase 49.4 Watering & Barring DownWater duration recorded. Ground conditions and observations captured. Photo evidence attached to record.
Phase 59.5 Explosives SearchBlasting licence holder confirmation. Misfire detection with escalation to PRO-0067 workflow.
Sign-OffSupervisor ConfirmationSupervisor name and digital confirmation. Record locked, timestamped, and saved. Retained 5 years per Record Control Policy.

The application includes offline mode — submissions queue locally when underground signal is unavailable and synchronise automatically when connectivity is restored. The system has a 15-second network timeout and automatically offers offline saving if the database is unreachable.

04
Pilot Plan

Proposed Section: One section confirmed by the Mine Captain and SHE Manager — recommended to be a section with an experienced and communicative Overseer Miner.

Duration: 30 calendar days from training completion.

Method: Parallel running — the digital procedure is completed for every re-entry alongside the paper form. No compliance obligations change. The pilot is additive only.

Important: Paper forms remain in use throughout the entire pilot period. Digital records are supplementary during the pilot phase. This ensures zero compliance risk regardless of pilot outcome.
