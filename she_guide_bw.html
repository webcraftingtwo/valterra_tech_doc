01
Accessing the Dashboard

The SHE dashboard is a separate URL from the miner app. Open it in any browser on your phone, tablet, or laptop. Sign in with your badge ID and password. Your role must be one of: SHE Manager, Shift Boss, Safety Officer, Section Manager, or Admin. Miner accounts cannot access the dashboard.

Recommended practice during the pilot: Open the dashboard at the start of every shift and keep it open on a surface computer or your phone throughout. The Live tracking tab refreshes automatically every 30 seconds. You will see every active miner, their current phase, gas readings, and zone without asking anyone.
02
Dashboard Tabs — What Each One Does
📊OverviewDaily Summary

Your starting point. Shows today's stats at a glance: total submissions, completed today, aborted re-entries, near misses, and timer exceedances. If the Timer Exceeded count is above zero, investigate immediately.

📋SubmissionsFull Procedure Records

Every completed and aborted re-entry procedure. Filter by date range, section, or status. Tap any record to see full detail — gas readings, crew members, ground observations, photos, supervisor name, and duration. Export individual records as PDF for formal reporting. Download all records as CSV for analysis.

  • Completed — full procedure submitted with supervisor sign-off
  • Aborted (Unsafe Gas) — gas readings exceeded legal limits, procedure correctly stopped
  • Records with ⚠ Timer Exceeded in the detail view exceeded the 30-minute re-entry limit
📡Live TrackingReal-Time Underground View

Shows every miner currently in progress — updated every 30 seconds automatically. For each active miner you can see their current phase, live gas readings colour-coded green or red, zone, and time elapsed. Any miner who has exceeded 30 minutes underground is highlighted in red with a badge showing how many minutes over the limit.

  • If a miner shows red gas chips — contact the Shift Boss for that section immediately
  • If a miner shows ⚠ EXCEEDED — radio the section, confirm miner is withdrawing
🆘SOS AlertsEmergency Response

Every SOS alert triggered underground appears here immediately with officer name, badge ID, zone, section, and time. Active alerts are shown in red. Tap any alert to open the resolve workflow — add resolution notes and mark resolved once the situation is under control.

An SOS alert requires immediate action. Do not wait. Contact the section Shift Boss, initiate emergency response procedures, and document your response in the resolve notes on the dashboard.
🪨Ground AlertsFOG Light Red Incidents

Every instance where a miner reported a RED FOG light in Phase 3. These are automatically escalated to Rock Engineering. Active ground alerts appear in red. The resolve workflow requires Rock Engineering confirmation notes before an alert can be cleared.

  • Only resolve a ground alert after Rock Engineering has physically inspected the area
  • Document the Rock Engineering representative's name and findings in the resolution notes
  • The section must remain barricaded until the alert is formally resolved
⚠Near MissesHazard Intelligence

All near-miss reports submitted by miners during re-entry procedures. Filter by incident type, severity, and section. Use this data to identify recurring hazards across sections and inform your weekly safety talk topics.

📈AnalyticsTrends & Compliance

Three analytical views for the SHE Manager:

  • Gas Trends — average daily gas readings per gas type over 30 days. Filter by section. A rising CO trend across multiple days indicates a ventilation issue before it becomes dangerous.
  • Submission Volume — daily completed vs aborted procedures. Low completion rate on a given day needs investigation.
  • Missed Re-Entry Detection — last 14 days showing submission counts per section per day. Days with zero records are flagged in amber. These are potential compliance gaps.
📋HandoversShift-to-Shift Knowledge

All shift handover records submitted by Overseer Miners at end of shift. Filter by section and shift type. Tap any record to see full handover detail including outstanding hazards, gas concerns, panels to avoid, and equipment status. Review these at the start of your shift before sending crews underground.

03
Recommended Daily Monitoring Routine
🌅
Start of Shift — 10 minutes
Check Overview stats from previous shift. Review Handovers tab for your section. Check if any SOS or Ground Alerts are still active and unresolved. Open Live tab and keep it running on a device throughout the shift.
⏱
During Shift — as needed
Monitor Live tab. Any miner showing red gas readings or timer exceedance needs immediate follow-up. If an SOS fires, respond to the alert on the dashboard immediately and initiate emergency response. New ground alerts must be escalated to Rock Engineering without delay.
📊
End of Shift — 15 minutes
Review today's submissions. Confirm all re-entries for the shift have a corresponding digital record. Check Near Misses tab for new reports. Export any submissions needed for formal records. Note any anomalies for the weekly review.
📈
Weekly — 30 minutes
Open Analytics tab. Check gas trends for any section showing upward movement. Review missed re-entry detection — any day with zero records needs explanation. Compile pilot progress notes for the pilot report. Share key observations with the Mine Captain.
04
Resetting a Miner's Password

If a miner cannot access the app because they forgot their password, you can reset it directly from the dashboard without involving IT.

Tap the 🔑 key icon in the top-right corner of the dashboard. Enter the miner's badge ID and a temporary password. The temporary password is displayed on screen — send it to the miner via WhatsApp before their shift starts. They use it to sign in and can change it from the app settings.

Do this before the shift starts — never underground. If a miner cannot sign in once they are underground, they must fall back to the paper form for that shift. A miner should never skip the SLAM procedure because of an app login issue.

06
Pilot Data Collection — Your Role

Your observations during the pilot are as important as the database records. Please keep a simple daily log noting:

  • How many re-entries were completed digitally vs paper only
  • Any technical issues miners reported
  • Miner feedback — positive and negative
  • Any safety observations that the app captured that paper may have missed
  • Any compliance gaps the analytics tab flagged

This qualitative data feeds directly into the pilot report and the recommendation to management on full deployment.

At the end of the 30-day pilot, distribute the anonymous miner feedback form to all participants. Their honest rating of the app versus paper is a critical part of the deployment decision.
